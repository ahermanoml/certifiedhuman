Why hire a human?

The human-agent handoff is the unsolved problem.

Everyone's building agents. Nobody's testing what happens when the agent needs to delegate to a person. How does it brief? How does it verify? How does it handle "I didn't understand"? You need to figure this out before your competitors do.

I'm the cheapest R&D you'll ever do.

You could hire a UX researcher for $150/hr to study human-agent interaction. Or you could pay me $12/hr to actually be the human in the loop. Live data, real friction, zero PowerPoints.

You'll discover problems you didn't know you had.

Your agent's instructions make sense to another LLM. They don't make sense to me. That gap between "clear to a machine" and "clear to a human" is where your product breaks in production. I find that gap for you.

Early movers win this one.

In 12 months every agent framework will need human-in-the-loop workflows. The teams who already know how that works — the friction, the payment, the verification — will ship while others are still designing specs. I'm your head start.
